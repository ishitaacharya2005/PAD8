PAD

Peripheral arterial disease(PAD)

It is a circulatory condition where narrowed arteries reduce blood flow to the limbs, most commonly the legs. This is typically caused by atherosclerosis, the buildup of plaque that hardens arteries. This leads to symptoms like leg pain and cramping during activity, and can increase the risk of heart attack or stroke. Early diagnosis and treatment help manage symptoms and prevent complications such as amputation.

Symptoms

PAD often causes leg pain, cramping, or numbness during walking due to reduced blood flow. Some people may notice slow wound healing or a cold sensation in the feet. In later stages, discomfort may continue even while resting.

Remedies

Regular walking exercises help improve blood circulation and reduce symptoms. Following a healthy diet, avoiding smoking, and controlling diabetes or blood pressure are essential. Doctors may also recommend medication to improve overall limb blood flow.
